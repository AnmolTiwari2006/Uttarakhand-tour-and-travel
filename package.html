top destinations

Sahstra Tal

Sahastra Tal, which means “Lake of Thousands,” is a pristine high-altitude lake located at an elevation of around 4,100 meters (13,500 ft) in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, near the border of Uttarkashi and Tehri districts. It offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan peaks and untouched natural beauty.

Panwali Kantha

Panwali Kantha (also called Panwali Kantha Bugyal) is a stunning high-altitude meadow in Tehri Garhwal district of Uttarakhand, with an average elevation of 3,368 m (11,050 ft) . This trek is known for its sprawling grasslands, colorful rhododendron blooms, serene forests, and panoramic views of the Char Dham peaks.

book now

Khait Parwat

Khait Parvat is a mystical mountain peak located near Ghansali in Tehri Garhwal, Uttarakhand, standing at an altitude of approximately 10,500 feet (3,200 meters). Locally known as the “Land of the Aanchari Yoginis” (nine fairy sisters), the region is surrounded by legends, natural beauty, and spiritual silence.

book now

Khatling Glacier

Khatling Glacier is a majestic high-altitude glacier located in the remote Bhilangana Valley of Tehri Garhwal, Uttarakhand. Sitting at an elevation of around 3,700 meters, it is the source of the sacred Bhilangana River, a tributary of the Ganga. Surrounded by towering Himalayan peaks like Jogin, Kirti Stambh, and Meru, the glacier offers a dramatic and untouched landscape.

book now

Kedarkantha

The Kedarkantha Trek is one of the most popular and beginner-friendly treks in Uttarakhand, located in the Govind Wildlife Sanctuary of the Uttarkashi district. Rising to an altitude of about 3,800 meters (12,500 feet), the summit offers breathtaking 360° views of snow-covered Himalayan peaks, including Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, and Black Peak.

book now

Har ki dun

The Har Ki Dun Trek is one of the most scenic and culturally rich treks in Uttarakhand, located in the Govind Pashu Vihar National Park in Uttarkashi district. Situated at an altitude of around 3,566 meters (11,700 feet), this trek takes you into a cradle-shaped valley surrounded by snow-covered peaks and alpine forests.

book now

Pindari Glacier

The Pindari Glacier Trek is one of the most accessible and rewarding glacier treks in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. Located in the Bageshwar district, this trek leads to the stunning Pindari Glacier, situated at an altitude of around 3,660 meters (12,000 feet) in the Kumaon Himalayas.

book now

Roopkund Trek

The Roopkund Trek is one of Uttarakhand’s most thrilling and mysterious high-altitude treks, located in the Chamoli district. Set at an altitude of around 5,029 meters (16,499 feet), Roopkund Lake is famously known as the Skeleton Lake due to hundreds of ancient human skeletons found at its edge — believed to be over 1,000 years old.

book now

Chandrashila Trek

The Deoria Tal – Chandrashila Trek is a short and scenic trek in Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand, combining serene lake views with breathtaking Himalayan panoramas. It starts at the calm Deoria Tal Lake (2,438 m), known for its crystal-clear reflections of snow-capped peaks, and culminates at Chandrashila Peak (3,690 m), offering one of the best sunrise views of the Indian Himalayas.

book now

Dayara Bugyal

The Dayara Bugyal Trek is one of the most picturesque and beginner-friendly treks in Uttarakhand, located in the Uttarkashi district. "Bugyal" means alpine meadow, and Dayara Bugyal, situated at an altitude of about 3,408 meters (11,200 feet), is one of the highest and most expansive meadows in India.

book now

Valley of Flowers

The Valley of Flowers Trek is one of the most enchanting and colorful treks in Uttarakhand, nestled in the Chamoli district within the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located at an altitude of around 3,658 meters (12,000 feet), this valley transforms into a vibrant carpet of over 500 species of wildflowers during the monsoon months.

book now

Harsil Valley

Harsil Valley, located in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, is a serene and untouched mountain valley nestled along the banks of the Bhagirathi River at an altitude of around 2,620 meters (8,600 feet). Often called the “Mini Switzerland of India,” Harsil is known for its dense deodar forests, apple orchards, peaceful riverside settings, and stunning Himalayan landscapes.

book now
